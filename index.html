1. What Is Membrane Roofing? The Best Match for Flat Roofs

Membrane roofing refers to a class of highly effective waterproofing systems specifically designed for flat or low-slope roofs. The three most commonly used membrane types are:

  • EPDM (Rubber Roofing): Known for its superior flexibility and UV resistance.
  • TPO (Thermoplastic Olefin): Offers reflective, energy-efficient performance.
  • PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride): Highly durable and resistant to chemical exposure.

Rooster Roofing's expert team recommends these systems due to their performance, longevity, and compatibility with Wisconsin's extreme weather conditions.

2. Engineered to Handle Wisconsin Weather

Membrane roofing systems are built to withstand the severe and varied climate of Southern Wisconsin. Whether it's heavy snowfall, intense rain, strong winds, or blazing summer heat, these roofs deliver reliable protection.

  • EPDM roofs resist cracking in freezing temperatures.
  • TPO and PVC roofs reflect sunlight, reducing indoor heat during the warmer months.

This superior adaptability reduces damage and helps maintain structural integrity over the long term.

3. Reliable Waterproofing to Prevent Leaks

Flat roofs can collect standing water, increasing the risk of leaks and interior damage. Membrane systems offer a strong advantage here: their seams are heat-welded or chemically bonded to create a watertight shield across the entire surface.

Rooster Roofing's experienced team ensures precise installation of all membrane layers, proper drainage angles, and high-quality flashing for ultimate protection from moisture infiltration.

4. Significant Energy Savings for Commercial Buildings

TPO and PVC membranes are designed to reflect sunlight, which can dramatically lower your building's cooling demands. The result:

  • Reduced HVAC costs
  • Greater indoor comfort for staff and visitors
  • Increased energy efficiency ratings

Many Rooster Roofing clients have reported noticeable reductions in their utility bills after upgrading to a membrane roofing system.

5. Lightweight Yet Tough: Structural Benefits

Membrane systems are lightweight compared to gravel-based or multi-layer built-up roofing. This puts less strain on your building's structure—a major benefit, especially for older commercial properties.

Despite their lighter weight, these materials are exceptionally tough, offering decades of reliable performance with minimal risk of structural issues.

6. Low Maintenance and Longevity You Can Count On

Once installed by skilled professionals like Rooster Roofing, membrane roofs require very little maintenance. With routine inspections and basic care, these roofs can last 20–30 years or more.

Any small issues—such as punctures or seam separation—can usually be fixed quickly and inexpensively, helping building owners avoid unexpected costs or shutdowns.
